Introduction

Tennis for Two is the first claim to be a video game, originating in 1958, 14 years before the existence of the more widely known game, Pong. Before arcades or hungry yellow dots, interested visitors waited in line at Brookhaven national library to take a peek at the creation of a small game. In this electronic game, two individuals use separate controllers connected to an analog computer and an oscilloscope as a monitor to ultimately play a simple game of tennis.

Creator

Nuclear physicist, William Higinbotham, decided to create his own science project once he realized that the science exhibits at the time were too arcane and created really no interest and interactivity to the audience. Reflecting on his idea, Higinbotham wrote, “It might liven up the place to have a game that people could play, and which would convey the message that our scientific endeavors have relevance for society." It totally worked. Hundreds of people lined up to be able to try out the game.

Legacy

After Higinbotham’s death, his own start-up science exhibit led to the production of the largely known games such as Pac-Man and Pong. While small, this was a harbinger to the video game industry worth billions of dollars. Even in his time, Higinbotham knew that science could lead to the production of fun to society.
